Beyond the Work

Outside the Office

Outside of work, I enjoy music, staying active, spending time with my dog Ace, and reading philosophy and world history, particularly the works of Martin Buber and Simone Weil. The habits and perspectives I have built across these areas shape how I think, and I try to bring that into how I work.


Music has shaped how I think about structure, flow, and adaptability. Playing the piano from an early age taught me to work iteratively: trying different approaches, refining until something clicks, and recognizing when a solution is both functional and intuitive. That instinct for iteration and precision carries directly into how I build data products.


Staying active through football, track, and time with Ace has built discipline, consistency, and situational awareness. These qualities translate directly into analytical work: maintaining focus through ambiguity, adjusting quickly when assumptions shift, and executing under pressure without losing care for quality.


Reading philosophy and history has shaped how I think about systems, context, and meaning. It pushes me to go beyond surface-level patterns and ask not just what the data shows, but why it looks that way and what that implies for the broader picture. Strong analysis requires that kind of depth.
